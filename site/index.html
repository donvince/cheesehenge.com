cheesehenge restoration project

a quest to find a lost page from the internet

Once upon a time, in an internet long ago, before google's mind reading level of search effectiveness, internet pages were diverse, unusual and funny.

Back then during the late 1990s or early 2000s I remember finding one specific comical page. The (reliable) route to finding the page was convoluted:

  • Open Netscape Navigator (RIP)
  • Visit the site of search engine altavista.com (RIP)
  • Type in an unusual search term
  • Click through to result page 12, 13 or 14ish
  • Then click the link 3 resuts down that page
Finding most pages on the internet back then was typically this level of difficulty: Wikipedia wasn't there with most of the answers, google handn't become the only place to search yet.

I find it somehow fitting that, having, for whatever reason, recalled this little gem of internet, I found it even more difficult to find. Google gave me huge amounts of answers, all exactly what I was asking for but none of which were that particular page I was hoping to find. AltaVista has been swallowed up by Yahoo! which was reluctant to give me the answer I was wishing for.

Incredibly, via a flash of luck, I found the address of the very site, I'd sent it to a friend via email. Alas, the site was no longer there "a 404 Not Found error was encountered while trying..."

All is not lost, for just such a tragic turn of events, the wayback machine was sent to help us.

Using the magic of the archive.org, you can see the archaeological remains of... the cheesehenge page!

The original page included a link to an oracle, which was at the ready to give you random javascript generated predictions. Sadly this was not captured by the wayback machine.
